2D Animation

In the term “2D animation” 2D stands for two-dimensional, meaning that 2D artists create images and characters in the two-dimensional format and enliven them with movement. This type of animation is regarded to be the traditional form of animation with plain, non-voluminous characters moving up, down, left, and right. How Is 2D Animation Created? There are many ways to create 2D characters and bring them to life. If you are a fan of old-school animation, then all you need is a pen/pencil and many sheets of paper (for black-and-white animation). Add a color palette to that set to get colored animation. So, here you are: draw your character on a sheet of paper, then duplicate it on another sheet and add a tiny change to it, then do it again and add a change. The typical speed of image shift in today's animation is 24 shots per second; so, after you have 24 sheets of paper with small changes added to each next one, you'll be able to display one second of animation.

What do 2D animators do?

Though 3D animation has become enormously popular at the end of the second millennium and still takes the lead in the world of animation today, 2D is far from dead. 2D animators are still in demand and perform a wide range of works for their clients including: Character design and creation Implementation of digital animation and video Creation of stop motion and web animation A variety of designs with the use of life drawing techniques.
